What are the steps involved in evaluating a sector?A. Some of the crucial steps involved in evaluating a sector include the past performance of the sector, the size of the sector and its contribution to the economy, the nature of the sector (defensive or high beta stocks), key developments in the sector, government policies, etc.2. Can a stock perform better even if the sector as a whole may be underperforming?A. Yes, there may be a case of a particular stock performing better despite the sector it belongs to underperforming. This can be due to factors like favourable news related to the company that pushes demand for the product or strategic alliance with significant partners that project a favourable long-term view for the company’s business, etc.3. What are the components of analysing a company?A. The key components of analysing a company include analysing the company profile, key financial ratios, demand and supply factors of the company and the sector as a whole, sector analysis, competition in the sector, and economic analysis. 4.
