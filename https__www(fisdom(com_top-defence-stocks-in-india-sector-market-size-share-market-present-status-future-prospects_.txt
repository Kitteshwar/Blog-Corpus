A country’s defence system has to be well equipped to counter any external and internal threat and ensure that the interest of the country is secure. The defence sector in India is one of the most prominent and strategically important sectors in the country. For many years, the defence sector was solely handled by the government. However, today there are many strong private players too that have contributed heavily to building a strong and reliable defence system for the country.   Table of Contents  hide   1 Sector overview   2 Government initiatives   3 Investments in the defence sector   4 Future projections   5 Top stocks in the Indian defence sector by market capitalization as of Apr 2022   6 Conclusion   7 FAQs Given below is a brief overview of the defence sector in India and the key details of the same.Sector overviewIndia is ranked to be the third largest spender in the defence sector and has about 3.7% of the global military spending. The financial year 2021 saw increased spending on the defence sector to the tune of Rs. 4.78 lakh crores which is approximately US $63 billion. The defence industry in India is owned by the government to the tune of approximately 80% and includes DRDO as well as its 50 Labs, 12 defence PSUs and 4 defence shipyards. The country is also hugely dependent on imports (approximately 50%) to meet its ever-increasing demand in the defence sector due to the ongoing tensions with two of our neighbours and the other border-related issues including internal security issues. 4ddxThe government is aiming to reduce its dependence on imports and is pushing to increase domestic production through various innovations and advancements in the defence sector. This initiative to increase domestic production is part of the Atma Nirbhar Bharat vision of the government. This has given a huge boost to the defence stocks in the country and they have seen a constant rise. The sector has seen a CAGR of 3.9% between the period 2016-2020 and has a production target of US$25 billion by 2025 which includes the target to achieve US$5 billion in exports by 2025. Government initiativesThe Government of India has been working diligently to work towards upgrading the defence system of the country in terms of its manpower as well as equipment. The various government initiatives in the defence sector include the following,The government has initiated the Defence Production and Export Promotion Policy 2020’ to promote the Atma Nirbhar Bharat vision and meet its target of US$25 billion by 2025.The government has also dedicated 7 PSUs in October 2021 that have been created through the restructuring of the Ordnance Factory Board (OFB) for the defence sector. This move was aimed to improve the efficiency and growth potential as well as innovation in the defence sector.The government is also promoting the MSME sector in the defence in the DRDO model and also has plans to establish a new defence park with state of the art infrastructure in Kerala.DRDO has also handed over the Licensing Agreements for ToT for 14 DRDO developed technologies to 20 industries at the Aero India 2021 in Bengaluru.Another initiative in the defence sector includes the plan of putting 101 defence items under the import embargo. This will provide a manufacturing opportunity for the military hardware.At the Aero India 2021, the defence Minister also announced the commitment to reduce the defence imports by a minimum of US$ 2 billion by 2022.The government estimates to procure a potential contract of approximately Rs. 4 lakh crore from the Indian Defence industry for the next 5 to 7 years, i.e. (between 2025-2027)Investments in the defence sectorThere have been many significant investments in the defence sector over the past years. Some of the significant investments are mentioned below.The FDI in the Defence Sector has been increased to 74% through the Automatic Route for companies looking for new defence industrial licenses and 100% by the government route. In November 2021, the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and IAF together successfully tested two indigenously made developed smart anti-airfield weapons.The government has entered into a bilateral agreement with Isreal to promote innovation and Research and Development (R&D) in the start-up sector and the MSMEs in both countries. This will help both countries use dual technologies in their respective country. The month of October 2021 was especially fruitful for the country as India signed and cleared contracts that were worth approximately Rs. 54,000 crores. These contracts will help the country enhance their military capabilities with the help of indigenously produced weapons and systems which can include tanks, transport planes, helicopters, drones and counter-drone weapons.The country also successfully launched the surface-to-surface ballistic missile, Agni-5 in October 2021. This missile has a capacity and a range of 5000kms and the degree of accuracy is very high.The Ministry of Defence signed a contract with the US Government, in October 2021, under Foreign Military Sale (FMS). This contract is for the procurement of MK 54 torpedo and expendables (chaff and flares) which will be used by the Indian Navy. The cost of this project is approximately Rs. 423 crore (US$ 56.53 million).Furthermore, the Ministry of Defence announced a deal to the tune of Rs. 22,000 crore (US$ 3.0 billion) in September 2021 with Tata-Airbus. This deal entails the procurement of 56 C-295 transport aircraft for the Indian Air Force (IAF). The Defence Minister Mr. Rajnath Singh also approved the launch of the Defence Testing Infrastructure Scheme (DTIS) in August 2021 with an outlay of Rs. 400 crore. This scheme aims at creating state-of-the-art testing infrastructure and boosting domestic defence & aerospace manufacturing. This will be a public-private partnership model and is expected to set up 6 to 8 new test facilities in the 5-year test scheme. In June 2021, the defence ministry also approved the proposal to construct 6 submarines. The cost of this project would be approximately Rs. 43,000 crores. Future projectionsThe country has been constantly increasing the defence budget and the current increase is pegged to have an increase of 19% in the Defence Capital Expenditure. India currently has about a 15% share in the global arms import and the value of defence exports in the last five years has increased from Rs. 1940.64 crores to Rs. 8434.84 crores in the year 20-21. The Defence Ministry has also set a target to achieve about 70% self-reliance in weaponry by the year 2027. This move has led to the creation of huge growth prospects for the industry giants and also the private players in this segment. Top stocks in the Indian defence sector by market capitalization as of Apr 2022The top stocks in the defence sector and their key details are mentioned below.Hindustan Aeronautics LimitedHindustan Aeronautics Limited is a PSU and is engaged in the business of manufacturing as well as maintaining aircraft and helicopters.
