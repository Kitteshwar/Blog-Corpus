How does trading happen in grey market?About Electronics Mart India LtdAs Covid-19 impact is seen to be fading away, people are gearing up for the festive season with surging demand in products such as clothing and electronics. This year, the consumer durable industry is expecting festive season sales to cover for the lost 2 years. Electronics Mart India is therefore timing its entry into the bourses very well.Apart from being the 4th largest consumer electronics and durable retailer in India, the company is also the largest player in revenue terms as it commands a dominant market position in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.The company offers a vast range of products such as air conditioners, televisions, smartphones, washing machines, etc. These products are sold through 112 stores spread across 36 cities in India. The company operates these stores under brand names such as Bajaj Electronics, Kitchen Stories, Electronics Mart, and Audio & Beyond. The company has built a strong market presence over three decades and with the funds gathered through the IPO, it is aiming to expand its store and warehouse network. Apart from this, the IPO proceeds will also be used to clear debt and fund working capital needs.Here are some of the other facts that investors should know about this company:The company was founded by Pavan Kumar Bajaj and Karan Bajaj as a proprietary company in 1980.The company has divided its business activities across 3 main channels including, retail, wholesale and e-commerce.Company financials Investors can refer the table below for a quick glance at the company’s financial performance:Mar-22Mar-21Mar-20Total Assets (Cr.)₹ 1,825₹ 1,523₹ 1,348Operating Revenue (Cr.)₹ 4,349₹ 3,202₹ 3,172PAT (Cr.)₹ 105₹ 59₹ 81Basic EPS₹ 3.46₹ 1.95₹ 2.72Diluted EPS₹ 3.46₹ 1.95₹ 2.72RONW17.42%11.92%18.84%Company strengths Some of the company’s strengths are:The company has tie-ups with over 70 electronic brands and sells them through over 6,000 stock keeping units (SKUs).From a store network of only 59 stores in 2019, it has managed to expand to 112 stores in 2022.The company targets densely populated neighbourhoods for its store locations.The company has maintained a strong cash flow, which it has further invested into business expansion.It operates 7 large-sized warehouses covering, on average, an area of 28,114 sq ft.It has been consistently paying its suppliers within agreed timelines which has allowed it to enjoy supplier discounts.The company’s business model is designed to allow operational flexibility for a long-term sustainable footprint.The company’s strong management and powerful brand name of Bajaj also work in its favour.Company weaknessesInvestors must take note of the below-mentioned drawbacks about the company:The company tends to adopt a cluster-based approach while opening new stores. This leads to concentration in certain geographies.The company has a high inventory obsolescence risk.The business approach requires significant working capital to carry out operations smoothly.The company has not attained a significant presence online and faces stiff competition in its e-commerce space.Risks involved in Electronics Mart India Ltd IPOHere are some of the top risks surrounding this company:Company has a dependency on supplies for its products. Therefore, delays and disruptions in its supply can adversely impact business operations.The company derives the majority of its revenues from a few brands. Thus, with any drop in the volume of these brands, the company’s profitability can be directly impacted.The industry can easily have new entrants and give rise to competition.With expansion in new areas, the company may have to face losses in certain areas of investment.Should you apply for Electronics Mart India IPO?Electronics Mart India Ltd has shown positive performance even during the Covid-19 period and managed to open new stores during this phase. This reflects the financial strength of the company.
