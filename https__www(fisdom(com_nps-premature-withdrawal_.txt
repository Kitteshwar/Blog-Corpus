Does the scheme have provisions for premature withdrawals and what are the conditions for the same? Given below are the provisions related to the withdrawal from the NPS account and its conditions. What is NPS? The NPS (National Pension Scheme) was introduced in 2004 by the Central Government under the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) and was made available for all citizens in 2009. The investors in the NPOS will be provided with a unique PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) and the objectives of the scheme were to provide regular income in old age, provide market-linked returns over a long-term investment horizon, and providing extending security coverage to all citizens in old age. The scheme also provides tax benefits in the form of deduction under Section 80C up to Rs. 1,50,000 and an additional Rs. 50,000 under section 80CCD(1B). The NPS essentially functions through two accounts i.e., Tier I and Tier I accounts. The former requires the accumulation of fixed monthly contributions which are locked till maturity, while in the latter, investors can park funds at their discretion and there is no restriction for withdrawals or deposits. Also Read: NPS or PPF – Where should you invest in?Terms for NPS withdrawal As mentioned above, there are no rules relating to withdrawal restrictions for Tier II in NPS, therefore, the withdrawal rules currently pertain only to the Tier I account of NPS. NPS withdrawal rules are based on various conditions like the sector that the investor belongs to, the type of withdrawal, the amount that can be withdrawn, and the timeline for such withdrawal. Given below are the various terms of withdrawal in Tier I of NPS. Also Read: NPS or Equities – Which is a better retirement tool?For Government employees Tier I withdrawal rules for Government employees are mentioned below. Maturity withdrawal rules An investor can withdraw their corpus at the age of 60 provided that,60% of the corpus can be withdrawn in a lump sum without any restriction which will be tax-free. This withdrawal can be postponed till the age of 70 years.Balance 40% to be invested in an approved annuity plan at the discretion of the investor. Investors are also free to add more funds to the annuity plans if they wish to do so. The annuity plan provides a monthly pension which will be taxable in the hands of the investor as per applicable slab rates.If the accumulated corpus is less than Rs. 5,00,000, there are no restrictions on the withdrawal of the entire corpus as per the above provisions. Voluntary exit withdrawal In case of a voluntary exit from the scheme (after completion of a minimum of 3 years from opening the NPS account), the following premature withdrawal rules shall apply.20% of the corpus can be withdrawn which will be taxable in the hands of the investor. Balance 80% has to be invested in an approved annuity plan as per the choice of the investor. Income from such a plan will also be taxable in the hands of the investor.If the accumulated corpus is less than or equal to Rs. 2,50,000, there are no restrictions on the withdrawal of the entire corpus. Death-related withdrawal In the event of the death of the investor, their nominee or the legal heir receives the full accumulated corpus without any restriction. For Private sector employees and citizens Tier I withdrawal rules for private sector employees and citizens are mentioned belowMaturity withdrawal rules The maturity withdrawal rules, in this case, are similar to that applicable to government employees. An investor can withdraw their corpus at the age of 60 provided that,Investors can withdraw 60% of their corpus in a lump sum without any restriction which will be tax-free. This withdrawal can be postponed till the age of 70 years for corporate employees and citizens too.The balance 40% corpus has to be invested in an approved annuity plan at the discretion of the investor and they can further allocate additional funds to the annuity plans if they wish to do so. These plans provide a monthly pension to investors which will be taxable as per their applicable slab rates.There are no restrictions on withdrawal if the accumulated corpus is less than Rs. 5,00,000Voluntary exit withdrawal In case of a voluntary exit from the scheme (after completion of a minimum of 10 years from opening the NPS account), the following premature withdrawal rules shall apply.Minimum 80% if the corpus has to be invested in approved annuity plans and income from the same will be taxable in the hands of investors at applicable slab rates.20% of the corpus can be withdrawn which will again be taxable.No restrictions on withdrawal if the accumulated corpus is less than or equal to Rs. 2,50,000.Death-related withdrawal Similar to the case of government employees, the nominees and the legal heir of the investors are entitled to get full corpus in the event of the death of the investor.Partial withdrawal rules of NPSApart from the above terms of withdrawal from the NPS account, subscribers are also allowed partial withdrawal from their corpus subject to conditions laid down in this regard. The details of the same are provided below. Conditions of partial withdrawal NPS has laid down specific conditions or reasons that warrant withdrawal from the corpus. These conditions are,Higher education of childrenExpenses related to the marriage of childrenFor purchase or construction of a new houseFor treatment of specified illnesses for self, spouse, and childrenTo meet the medical and incidental expenses on account of any disability or incapacitation of the investorFor any self-development activities like skill development or re-skillingTimelines for partial withdrawalsApart from the above conditions for withdrawal, there are rules relating to the timeline for submitting the partial withdrawal application. These conditions are specified hereunder. Application for partial withdrawal can be submitted only after a minimum of 3 years from opening the NPS account.The request for partial withdrawal can be submitted only 3 times.The minimum time gap between any partial withdrawal request should be at least 5 years. The amount that can be withdrawnThe conditions relating to the amount that can be withdrawn are mentioned below.Investors can withdraw a maximum of 25% of the contributions made by them into the NPS account. Investors can withdraw only the principal portion of the corpus and not the accrued interest. ConclusionNPS was introduced to provide better retirement solutions to the citizens of the country. The scheme also understands the need for immediate financial assistance in several cases and, therefore, has chalked out detailed rules that can facilitate the easy withdrawal from the scheme while at the same time safeguarding the interest of the subscribers. FAQsCan a person get a loan against their NPS account? Subscribers can withdraw funds from their NPS account subject to the applicable rules, however, a loan against an NPS account is not permissible as yet.Can investors of Tier II accounts withdraw from their NPS corpus?There are no restrictions on the withdrawal of corpus from Tier II NPS accounts.Does NPS allow withdrawal of more than 60% corpus upon reaching the age of 60 or 70 years?A person can withdraw the full corpus or 100% of the corpus upon reaching the age of 60 or 70 years if the accumulated funds in the account are less than Rs. 5,00,000.Can investors do partial withdrawal for Covid?Yes. Investors are allowed to partially withdraw from their NPS accounts for the treatment of covid under the condition treatment of specified illnesses.When will the partially withdrawn amount be credited to the investor’s bank account?The amount of partial withdrawal will be credited to the subscriber’s bank account (as per the details provided at the time of subscription) in T+4 days where T is the date of partial withdrawal application.
