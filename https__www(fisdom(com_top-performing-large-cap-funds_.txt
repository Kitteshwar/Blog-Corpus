Every stock market investor must aim to gather sufficient knowledge of investment categories, types, strategies, etc before making an investment. Stocks are mostly categorised as per their market capitalisation, also known as market cap. Market cap is a reflection of the company’s size. The three main stock classifications according to market cap are large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap. A company’s size is a crucial factor to be considered while choosing stocks for investment. A large-sized company brings some unique opportunities as they are well established companies and therefore, many investors prefer large-cap fund investments. If you are keen on exploring large cap fund investments, here is all the information you will need to begin with. Large cap companies are those which are well-established in the business segment and have a long positive track record. These companies have good corporate-governance and wealth-generation is often a slow and steady process. ​ Why invest in Large cap funds?Since large-sized companies have a steady performance, investors can expect regular dividend payouts. As far as the risk-return is concerned, large-cap funds have mostly steady returns with a lower risk factor as compared to mid-cap and small-cap funds. Here are some features which make large cap funds an ideal investment choice:Large cap funds normally see significant capital growth apart from offering stability to investors. The investment value of large-cap funds is usually unaffected by frequent market volatility. These offer some level of security to investors as the NAV of such funds is mostly stable.Investors who are looking for diversification with moderate to low risk can opt for large-cap funds.Investors can expect returns in the long term. Here are some of the important things to consider before investing in these funds:Large Cap funds are not risk-proof: Large Cap equity funds do have several risks that are mainly market-related. However, these risks are significantly moderate as compared to small-cap or mid-cap funds.Consider the expense ratio: Large Cap funds do have expenses related to fund management. This affects the expense ratio of the fund. Hence, while choosing a fund, look for a lower expense ratio which can help in fetching higher profits.Work better for a longer investment horizon: Large Cap equity funds can fetch good returns in the medium to long run. It makes sense to remain invested for least three to five years in large cap funds so as to get reasonable returns.Are subject to Capital Gains Tax: Large Cap funds are subject to the same tax treatment as any other equity asset.
